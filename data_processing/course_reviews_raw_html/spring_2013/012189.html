I sincerely hope for every student interested in American Studies that they revamp this course. Regardless of your political ideology, the rampant bias in the class is criminal for an introductory course to a field that is self-defined as broad-based. Only Professor DeLue is decent (albeit flaky) while Professor Hartog is an ideologue and Professor Cheng beats her agenda and opinions into you (for lack of a more professional phrasing). The greatest irony is that some of the course's final readings address and critique the rampancy of politicized bias in American Studies, even as the professors support and continue that so eloquently lambasted trend. Aside from the bias, I feel betrayed by the actual topics of study. For a course titled "America: Then and Now" there was remarkably little of either the "now" or a comparison between "then" and "now." As most of the material originates in the 20th century, if not solely its second half, then there is arguably little of the "then" as well. In summary, history seemed to be laid by the wayside in favor of what amounted to sociology. The topics are certainly worthy of study, though they exist beyond the officially stated goals of the course as many of the phenomena had remarkably little effect on the trajectory of the United States compared to events such as the American Revolution, Jacksonian Democracy, Reconstruction, the Great Depression, and World War II. Despite the majority of the discussed material coming in the post-war years (1950s through the present), almost nothing was said about the Cold War! In short, do not take this class unless they revamp it and replace or rein in the professors.
