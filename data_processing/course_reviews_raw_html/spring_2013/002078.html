A solid core CS course. Unless you have a strong reason not to take it, I would strongly advise all people interested in tech to take this course. I came into this class after not taking COS 318, so I definitely felt at a disadvantage on the assignments. First off, Freedman is an awesome lecturer, he runs fast through the material so make sure you are paying attention and can type fast. Next, the assignments are absurd. What you make really isn't that interesting, they take for-freakin'-ever (that's typical C programming though), and the assignments HAVE ERRORS IN THEM! I cannot believe there were actual errors in the assignment that weren't fixed until someone told the instructors on Piazza. That removed ALL incentive to try and start the assignments early because you'd hit a snag somewhere and it would be completely not your fault. The response we got was "you guys should figure out these problems yourselves." This is why people who took COS 318 were at an advantage, they were used to these kinds of situations. Our year the assignments were made much easier, but they still took at least 2 all-nighters (it took me 4 straight all-nighters to finish the router assignment, look forward to that one). The final exam was fully comprehensive, do not only study the 2nd semester. Outside of absolutely horrible assignments and really hard exams, this was incredibly informative and useful in lecture. VERDICT: prepare to sleep in the Fishbowl (computer room in friend) for the assignments, but the information taught in lecture is really cool and interesting. Recommend.
