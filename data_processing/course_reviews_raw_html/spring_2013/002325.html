Not going to lie, this course is easy. It's the easiest departmental/required-pre-engineering course I've taken so far here. And for those who strongly dislike the "memorize equations and plug and chug" methods of learning, you will strongly be displeased. Yet for those looking to understand the straightforward basics for fluids without taking on too much of a load, it's great. And because the lecturer, Professor Smits, is very engaging, the course is interesting even though it's easy. For those in MAE, remember that you will most likely take this course simultaneously with MAE 206 and MAE 224, two courses which DO NOT use the "plug and chug" method and are much more abstract and challenging, so this course actually becomes a nice relief in a way. You may be bored at times (i.e. the problem sets sometimes are so easy the preceptors have to separate students by taking off points for mislabeling graphs), but in my mind, the easy is good and Professor Smits is worth the time you put in.
