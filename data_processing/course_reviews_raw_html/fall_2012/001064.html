Student Comments:
What advice would you give to another student considering taking this course?
As someone who began taking this course having taught myself Chinese for about a year, I would say that the majority of what I learned in this course was grammar. I already had a knowledge of around 700 characters, which made each week's new words much easier to commit to memory, as I often knew more than half of them. The homework for this course usually didn't take me more than around a couple of hours per week. For someone who is coming into this course having absolutely no knowledge of Chinese, I would say that it is definitely going to be time-consuming. If you are really motivated to learn, then you should find this course enjoyable and rewarding. For people who take the extra effort outside of class to engage in conversation with other students who know Chinese, or to attend Chinese tables, you will find that you will progress faster and find the course more manageable. The teachers are very accessible, approachable, and dedicated to tailoring their teaching method to each student. I would recommend that you take advantage of the opportunity to ask questions, attend individual sessions, and get to know your teacher. For a beginner, this course is very intensive, but certainly worth it for someone who aims to speak Chinese well.
