If you're interested in biology but want to do more than just memorize, this is a great course. It would be best if you were still fairly familiar with AP chemistry and Calculus, as well as a bit of physics as there will be some math in the course, not all that much though. Major quantitative topics include looking at diffusion to analyze kinetics, free energy changes and binding, and Poisson statistics in relation to cancer. Also a big part of this class is understanding the classical experiments behind biology and how we know what we know, so expect to read some papers and understand how they arrived at their conclusions. Exams also have this emphasis, and might ask you to create an experiment similar to one looked at in class, or to predict the results if a given mistake had been made. The material was really interesting, and I would strongly recommend this course if you at all remember Math and Chemistry from high school and want to know some of the why and how behind biology. Workload is relatively light, with weekly problem set/lab report hybrids which are really short. Exams are not very difficult if you remember AP Biology, pay attention in lectures and at at least looked at the assigned readings.
