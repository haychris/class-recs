This course is a challenge. I'm not going to sugar-coat it in any other way - it's really one of those courses where you have to "get" the material *and* put in a significant amount of work on problem sets and concept comprehension based on textbook reading just to do passably well. If you're willing to work hard and you have a decent high school background in Chemistry, though, the course can be a real blast. It is materials chemistry, so to that extent you'll learn interesting topics about the useful chemical properties of different substances that you wouldn't learn anywhere else. If you have a weak background in chemistry from high school, though, don't take this course without having done some supplementary work on Chemistry during the summer. Unless you do nothing but study chemistry, you will almost invariably fall behind. And I hate to say it, but if you're pre-med and watching your GPA, this may not be the course for you either. There's a reason that pre-meds usually take 201-202 rather than 207-202, and it's consistent with what I've heard from my numerous friends in 201 - 201 grades easier, it spoon-feeds you more (thank you Alex, their system of online exercises), and prior extensive knowledge of chemistry is not assumed. That being said, if you're a chemistry person and looking for an intellectual challenge, 207 is the way to go!
