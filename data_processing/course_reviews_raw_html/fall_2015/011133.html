aaa. Prof Belcher: So, lectures-wise, it can get pretty boring. And she takes attendance in lectures, which count for a major part of your grade, so no skipping lecture. That being said, it's easy to fall asleep in lecture, but you're not missing much if you do. Not a bad professor, just talks monotone and super-slow in lecture. Also makes provocative points that you will be sure to disagree with. Readings: are very good. I personally enjoyed reading every page of these readings. There are a lot of books, but don't be intimidated by the list. One of them is a movie, another is a graphic novel. And you only really need to read in-depth for 2 of them, for the 2 essays you write. Critical Essays: You write 2. One in first 6 weeks, another in last 6 weeks. You get to pick the reading you write it on, so do read ahead to see what's the best book to write on. You want to analyze a short story of 250 pages of reading? My preceptor was a lenient grader for me (or really liked my writing, but I don't consider myself an excellent writer). 5-6 pages, double-spaced. Standard stuff. Creative Essay: You write 1. >12 pages, no max limit. Really fun project, getting to explore yourself and understand who you really are. It's open-ended, in the construction of a memoir. Overall: A great class. Lectures are mandatory (count for a substantial portion of your grade), can be a bit boring. But on the upside, as I quote Prof Belcher, "I have no limits on A's. Any student who does well will be graded well."
