8:30am is rough, but Prof. Graham is a great lecturer, enthusiastic and very organized. This is not a traditional immunology class memorizing a ton of molecules and names but more of an evolutionary class talking about how the molecular immunology stuff translates to the population level. There were a lot of interesting topics related to infectious diseases, immunopathology, vaccines, etc. There are two short "creative" papers, a final paper on any topic you choose, a take-home midterm, and take-home final.
