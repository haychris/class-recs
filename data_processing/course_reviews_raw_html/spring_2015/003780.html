TAKE IT
TAKE IT! I'm going to start with the negatives, but read through this whole evaluation to get my positives at the end. I was disappointed by this semester as compared to first semester. HUM 218-219 was poorly organized in several ways: readings were not listed accurately on the syllabus, and we would only receive exact page selections in scattered emails containing Blackboard announcements; nobody told the students in advance that they needed to distinguish between specific works of certain authors like Montaigne and Leibniz for the final exam; preceptors especially at the beginning of the semester often had students give very general or fluffy comments on the texts, or dominated the conversation by essentially turning precept into lecture; the papers were spaced badly so that originally there was no Dean's Date paper and they all would have been due two weeks apart, crammed into February through April; the person giving the philosophy lectures (Daniel Cloud) was ineffective, etc. In spite of all these concerns, I greatly enjoyed HUM 218-219 for the many excellent lectures, precepts and readings whose high quality carried over from first semester, and I felt that I learned and grew exponentially by not only taking the first semester, but also completing the entire sequence. If you can only take one or the other, fall HUM is much more important and more formative--with more essential texts and, in 2014-2015, better professors and precepts--than spring HUM, and students are also more engaged and on top of their readings in fall HUM. The drop-off in student preparation reduces the quality of discussions in the spring, but this decline is compensated in part by the benefit of better knowing your classmates and therefore being able to anticipate and respond to their comments more. If at all possible, I would recommend taking both fall and spring HUM; the texts you read, the people you befriend and the conversations you have are really a magical experience that you cannot get elsewhere at this University. I also found that I grew greatly as a writer throughout HUM; the papers came much more easily to me after the first or second one of the spring. I feel that I am leaving the HUM Sequence with much better writing skills, improved speaking skills, appreciation for and much greater awareness of the trajectory of Western culture, friends who are like me interested in humanistic inquiry, and a sharper and more insightful mind. Anyone who likes to read and has an interest in the HUM Sequence should sign up: it will change your life, and I am so thankful for this opportunity.
