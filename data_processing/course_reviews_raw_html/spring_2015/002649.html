I have mixed feelings about this class. On the one hand, take it: the readings are phenomenal, the precept discussions are generally good, the papers are short. What could be better? On the other hand, the lectures. Wow, those lectures. Just a bunch of pseudo-philosophical bull where Professor Chihaya rambles about postmodern narrative theory and "privileging the reader" and "authoritative performativity" and "raising important questions." We raised so many important questions, and never bothered to discuss any of them. The prof seemed lost up there at times, like being an english prof lecturing to 60 students puts her under the pressure of a brain surgeon. She'll hem and haw for a while and before you know it you can't even remember what question you raised and are about to not try to answer. These lectures are atrocious. On the other hand, you don't have to go to any of them. So take the class?
