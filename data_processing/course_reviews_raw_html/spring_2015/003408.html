I strongly recommend this very rewarding but very demanding class. Jordan could be the best lecturer I've had at Princeton. He's engaging, witty, and makes history come alive. That being said, I would avoid his precept. He's an extremely harsh grader and tends to ramble about off-topic issues. Because the course's emphasis is on the development of governmental institutions and common law, the material is largely theoretical, so it's quite hard to predict what will be on exams; this is not a typical history course that focuses on events, people, etc. Be sure to take very good notes in lecture, as the readings don't really go in depth about law and governance in the second half of the semester. Overall, definitely take this course if you are interested in English history or want to better understand the foundations of American govt. You will learn a lot, but be ready to work.
I think any history concentrator should take this course, and anyone interested in going on to law school or a career in politics should take this course. This is not a course to take for an easy HA.
