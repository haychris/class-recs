Student Comments:
What advice would you give to another student considering taking this course?
CBE 346 is the core CBE class. I was amazed at the resources that are used on this class. Just looking at the lab, the amount of TAs, teachers, and materials used on this lab is really pretty cool. That being said, it was a great class - because it showed me that I don't want to be a chemical engineer. It is sad that this class comes so late in the CBE program because it is really the first introduction to what chemical engineers do. And frankly, measuring hot fast rods heat up or how dye moves through a polymer isn't how I imagine most people want to spend their jobs. In terms of grading, the course is so subjective it is comical. The difference between professors is huge and whoever is the grader makes all the difference. Another huge difference is the TA you get for each lab, some are very helpful, others not so much so it really influences your grade. But back to subjectivity, you will work hard for a presentation to have it be argued that you had lines on a graph where there shouldnt be and one of the two professors didn't like that. It's sad that most of the time, the professors would rather not help the student and criticize them for not understanding material during a presentation or lab report instead of trying to help the student understand the material before hand. It's almost like the professors specifically don't try and help the student so they will be able to criticize them later. My best advice is to choose a partner that you know you will get along with and will get the work done and don't take it too seriously. You will be working late nights, you will spend way too much time on your lab reports, you will lose time you normally spend on other assignments for this class, and yet, you will be criticized for things you would never know or have thought to think about and you will be criticized for stylistic things that don't actually matter. So, have fun in the lab, order pizza for late night lab sessions, and blast some music.. and DON'T take it too seriously. Pretty soon it will be over and you won't have to worry about if your error determination was displayed just too much or too little (there is no correct amount you will find out, only too much or too little).
