Amazing class, highly recommend it, even as non-English major. I took the class because I wanted an interesting class without a ridiculous workload that would 1) teach me how to enjoy literature more and 2) expand the range of the books I've read. I loved every second of this class, although it would have been very challenging for someone with no real English background if I had taken this for a grade. Gleason was an amazing lecturer -- he really made the texts come alive, and showed me how much more enjoyable literature can be when you read it with an expert. I had always gotten the impression that academic English destroyed the aesthetic and leisure value of literature, but Gleason proved me wrong. The work was graded very strictly, which was good -- it made me realize that my snobby comments about English majors' easy grades were dead wrong. I'm really glad I took this P/D/F and would recommend doing so unless you have prior experience in English. Hadn't read most of the books, and they were awesome. I've been inspired to read more of these authors in my own time! I was fortunate enough to be in Gleason's precept. Gleason was the best preceptor I had in my four years at Princeton -- he kept the precept engaged, and if he ever went over time, I was as surprised as he was. He's just so incredibly interested in the subject, and genuinely cares about what we have to say. Every preceptor should take lessons from him.
