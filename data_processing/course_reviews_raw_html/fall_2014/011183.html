I recommend this unique course for its cross-scale approach and balance of specific content with bigger themes. Accordingly, I feel like I will be able to take more away from this class than many other biology courses that just focus on details. Professor Graham is fantastic--smart, articulate, and accessible--and the 8:30am lectures are among the most engaging and easy-to-follow that I've had at Princeton. As a non-EEB major, I felt like I learned a satisfying amount about evolutionary biology. The workload was fair, with two small creative papers, a term paper, and a midterm and final (both un-timed take-home, open-note).
I would highly recommend this course especially if you're not really interested in learned all the specific molecular mechanisms and structures of each molecule. You will instead learn how immunology is integrated on an evolutionary standpoint, which is a very different and new perspective worth exploring.
I would take this course if you are a MOL major or an EEB major with a strong molecular biology understanding.
