Taking ORF 309 was the second most valuable educational experience in my life, second only to being potty trained at the age of two. I would enthusiastically recommend this course to anyone who thinks that doing stuff with numbers can be fun. So what makes ORF 309 so great? 1. It's useful. I'm a junior in the COS department, and I took this course because I wanted to understand machine learning. I had taken COS 424 (machine learning) the previous spring, and had felt embarrassed that I couldn't follow many of the proofs that required manipulating probability distributions. It's one thing to be *acquainted* with the laws of probability, but it's another thing to really feel them in your bones. ORF 309 makes you fluent in probability. Also, if you're interested in a job in quantitative finance (or at least want to leave the option open) this course would come in handy for interviews. 2. It's entertaining. Probability questions are just really, really fun to solve. 3. It's not that hard. Ramon van Handel is an incredible teacher and a gifted explainer. He makes probability easy. Coming into this course, I was uneasy, because I had often heard ORF 309 compared to COS 340, which was for sure a hard class. This is not a good comparison. ORF 309 is much easier. If you put in the effort (go to office hours, collaborate with a group), there's no reason why you can't get every homework question right. And the exams generally have straightforward questions. All around, this was an amazing course.
