What could I possibly say? If you're interested in math as a major, take this course. If you're even considering it, take this course. Even if you don't like math that much, take this course. In general, take this course. I had no (I mean, literally 0) experience with proofs before this course. And yet, the impeccable lectures and great textbook gave them a beauty I had never seen before. That being said, be prepared to work. And I mean, a LOT, especially in the first few weeks. These problem sets are not for the faint-hearted. You can take this class even if you're not interested in a math major/minor, but you have to keep up with the effort of the math majors to survive the curve. Be prepared for at least 15-20 hours per problem set. If you want to do better than average, start the problem sets early in the week and go out of your way to ask for help when you're stuck. It's absolutely possible to pass this class with a respectable grade, so don't be terrified, but definitely don't try to cram a week's work into one or two days.
