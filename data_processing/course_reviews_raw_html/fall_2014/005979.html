If you are exceptionally good at having objective, academic discussion, the course is a great opportunity for practice with many different viewpoints and philosophical schools. If, like me, you are truly passionate and firm in your beliefs on any of the topics -- war, race, gender, the death penalty, the environment, etc. -- then the course will require an exhausting amount of forced neutrality. There is an almost endless wealth of ideas to be gained from the course, but to access them requires an incredible amount of time and patience for potentially disagreeable stances. It is easy to glide through the course without putting in much work, but there are better EMs to do that with.
