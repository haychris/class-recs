This course is a good LA if you have some music background. I had prior experience with piano and music theory (ABRSM grade 8/grade 5) and found the course to be fairly easy, though much of the later material about harmony was new to me. The textbook was barely used and is not worth getting since the professor's notes are adequate and posted online, but this may change in future semesters since this is Dennehy's first time teaching it. Weekly assignments and the three quizzes were straightforward, and lectures were informative but started to become less organized and focused towards the end of the semester (just listening to various songs). We were given a lot of freedom in composing our midterm and final projects (literally just compose a song around a certain length), which was nice. When I was shopping the course, I was initially not so thrilled about composing, but it turned out to be not so bad. Professor Dennehy is a nice guy and is pretty chill. His precepts were also the best for learning material - the other preceptors seemed to ask questions or analyze pieces all the time. In short, a good LA for "baby music theory" if you prefer to learn some stuff about music rather than memorize composer birthdays as in MUS 103. However, prior music experience (reading music, keys, intervals, chords) and some piano ability for composing is definitely recommended and advantageous.
