I had Antonio Ache as a professor for Fall of 2014-2015. I think if you are thinking about whether to take this class, you are probably trying to decide between taking EMP or not. While I think the physics portion of EMP is much better than the math, it is worth taking the math class so that you can have the great experience from physics too. While the instruction was not great (i.e. I had to look most stuff up in the book which was pretty clear if you took the time to read it), the professors host problem sessions every week before the problem set which is really great to not only get your problem set done (because frankly, the students are sometimes better at explaining the content than the professors) but also make friends! And from what I have heard from people in regular MAT 201, the instruction is no better and the tests are harder...of course this may not necessarily be the case next year. Another benefit in taking this course is the added flexibility of the professors because it is taken jointly with physics. One time, we had a big take-home quiz in math due the next day as well as a physics pset due the same day so Vikram Duvvuri, our physics professor, pushed the deadline for our physics problem set back a day so we could all focus on the math quiz. Of course, if you were not in EMP and just took MAT 201 and PHYS 103, you would not have this type of flexibility. Also, by the end of EMP, you really know nearly all 60 people in the program and because you all suffer over the same problem sets together, they become your closest friends.
