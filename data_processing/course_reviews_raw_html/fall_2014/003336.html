This class gives you a good sense of what Professor Grafton's lectures are like. The class also exposes you to more content than you can digest (condensation of ~2000 years of history into one semester), with lots of opportunities to learn how to read primary sources like a historian. Even though the pace of the readings was very fast for me, I really appreciated having exposure to thinkers like Abelard, Aquinas, Pico, Machiavelli, and More. It's quite rare for people to study such a wide span of human history in a short amount of time. Focus on the readings you and your preceptor care about. Ask your preceptor and Grafton for advice on your final paper throughout the writing process. Although the class is huge, Grafton and his graduate minions make a great effort to help students on an individual basis.
