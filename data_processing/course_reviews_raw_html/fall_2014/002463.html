ELE 206 is a great course in which you'll learn how all digital logic works. This is calculators, computers, etc. After taking this course, you will know enough to build processors in minecraft like they do on the Youtube videos, or how to implement a TOY machine (from COS 126) in hardware. The workload is as much as for any other course with a lab, and is nothing particularly difficult in that respect. The final project, which is to design and implement a general-purpose processor, however, is quite substantial, so you may be spending the end of the semester in the ELE lab for many days working on it. Non ELE/COS majors will also find it useful to take the course as a matter of subject interest. I recommend COS 126 be taken first since you will be at a disadvantage without it, though it is not necessary since Malik goes over everything you need to know, right from the beginning. Having an aptitude for quantitative reasoning will benefit you immensely in the course, since quite a lot of it is required.
