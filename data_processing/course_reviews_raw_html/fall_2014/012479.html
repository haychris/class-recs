Student Comments:
What advice would you give to another student considering taking this course?
Absolutely a great class, Prof Tarnita is amazing!
Be comfortable with the math. The course is much more interesting and beneficial to the student if they understand basic math concepts as derivatives, exponential functions, and geometric series.
Difficult course so don't take it if you already have a hard schedule. If you want to learn how to model biological, medical, or any other problems, then this is a great class to take. You'll learn a lot of broad and useful techniques to use as well as some computer programming models to implement.
If you are an EEB major who has little math or computer experience/skill, be careful taking this course. It's great, but it will require a lot of work.
If you are considering a career in research within MOL and EEB, I would highly recommend this course. As an EEB major, this course reinforced what I was learning in other classes while providing a whole new way of conceiving of ecological phenomena that I learned about in 309 and 321. EEB background is very helpful, but not entirely necessary. If you can think in terms of populations and population dynamics you should be fine. Rudimentary knowledge of linear algebra and calculus is a definite plus, though Prof Tarnita is willing to work with you if you are in a rut.
If you have any interest in mathematical biology, or using mathematics to get at other questions in biology, go for it! It is a reasonable workload and accessible to anyone with a modest calculus background (AP Calc AB would do). Start thinking about your final project early - it is very manageable but it is a good idea to have something in mind that you would like to work on.
It helps to know matlab / some coding because you'll need it for simulations. Lectures are helpful especially since there isn't a textbook.
It's interesting and not a lot of work but the final report is more work than everything else.
Prof Tarnita is one of the most accomplished and down-to-earth lecturers you will likely ever have - she's reason alone to take the class. It teaches you a new way of thinking, isn't unduly onerous and if you're reasonably comfortable with algebra you'll have a good time!
Professor Tarnita is extremely nice and helpful. She makes difficult subject matter interesting and manageable.
Professor Tarnita is one of the best professors I've had so far at Princeton. She is extremely encouraging and helpful. She doesn't just want you to learn something, she wants you to understand it and be able to replicate it. This is very important in a class about building mathematical models. She is very friendly and extremely approachable even though she is super smart. The lectures were pretty interesting, though the problem sets were time-consuming. Andrew's precept and office hours are definitely necessary - and he is also really amazing. Professor Tarnita is also very open to suggestions on how to improve the class. That being said, this was one of the most difficult courses for me because I had only taken MAT 103. The course recommends a year of calculus. There is also some linear algebra involved. If you don't have a solid mathematical background, know what you're getting into and be prepared to work. Office hours really helps. But really, if you're interested in the topic (esp. species interactions and population dynamics), take it. It's a unique class.
