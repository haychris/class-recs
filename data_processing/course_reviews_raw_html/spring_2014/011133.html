Great class and perhaps the easiest EM. The workload includes easy weekly readings (the reading list is amazing, and if you have a ton of work one week, it's not completely necessary to do the reading if you're not writing a paper that week) and optional weekly viewing, two papers on two of the weekly readings that you select in precept at the start of the course, and a creative memoir for the final due on Dean's Date. No midterm or exams. Overall, very manageable. The class is probably the most racially colorful at Princeton (see the title and listing of the course), so you get to meet a lot of diverse, cool people. Even if you do not think of yourself as transnational, you will learn and contribute to the class and perhaps find that the definition of transnational is broader than you think. Lectures are not good, but note of warning: Prof. Belcher takes lecture attendance seriously. Precepts are wonderful. Taylor Eggan is a wonderful preceptor, but most of them seem to be great, as well. I'd highly recommend this class to people looking for an easy EM and everyone else, too.
