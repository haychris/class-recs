What advice would you give to another student considering taking this course?
Be prepared to do a lot of work on your own.
Be prepared to think creatively and explore the options available to you in the architecture building, like the modelling software and the woodworking shop. Come to each class with work you've done from the past couple days so you can get feedback from the instructors, as they are your best resource. I'd recommend this course if you're genuinely interested in architecture and are willing to work hard. You won't get much out of this course if you don't put the effort in.
Definitely take this if you're interested in architecture. Be prepared for some independent learning though and be sure to budget the time you spend on projects.
Don't get paranoid that you have to fully finish everything; drawings, model etc, just work on having a nice and clear presentation that represents your idea- and then stop and go to bed.
Don't take this course as a freshman. Take it only if you're sure you want to concentrate in Architecture, after having taken a couple of other courses in the department.
If you are in ARC or CEE, this is a course that you really need to take. You might not like it, but you should still take it.
If you like design and are actually considering architecture as a major take it, otherwise it would be too time consuming just to take for fun.

Ask them and the arch juniors/seniors/grad students for feedback and software help. If you're reading this over summer, learn Rhino. And possibly Illustrator.
