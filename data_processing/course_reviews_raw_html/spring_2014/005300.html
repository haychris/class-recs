I took this class out of general Washington, politics, WWS interest. Probably not the best idea. The class focuses about half on constitutional interp and half on civil liberties (despite the course name). George is very good as lecturer (he wanders around the room without notes and is almost amazingly unbiased) but the 80 minute format is too long and this is not a lecture that will keep you awake unless this is your thing - not really a general interest course. The readings are half court cases and half philosophical pieces - I honestly regret doing most of the readings, esp the philosophical ones. Much of the class doesn't. I had George as a preceptor, which was not a good experience. He treated it as a 2nd (again 80 minute) lecture, was highly partisan, and didn't really allow for discussion (in the few times he would let someone who hasn't already taken con interp speak, he would shoot down their idea/opinion instead of pushing them to a better understanding). It was nice to have only one lecture a week though, 80 min lecture and 80 min precept. There is no weekly hw other than readings and only a midterm and final paper. The midterm paper was fun (or at least creative) - you got to decide an imaginary court case.
I would definitely recommend this course to other students.
