Take Sorensen! This class is excellent, as long as you stay on top of your material, as with any class. Lectures are Tuesday and Thursday. Everyday after lecture, spend 15 minutes over lunch reviewing what was just thrown at without you. You won't be confused. Problem sets are due Thursday so work on them after Tuesday, at which point you should know what to do for the problem sets. Do a more in-depth review sometime on Friday-Sunday, for 1-2 hours on the material you learned for the week. Start studying for exams 5-7 days in advance -- first by reviewing the 7-8 lectures that will be tested, and then by doing all the practice exams that are available. Save the last day for going over mistakes, redoing problems, finalizing your cheat sheets, and quickly skimming through the lectures again. Make sure to understand the examples he puts in the notes, especially those ones that seem complex and just "challenge problems." Cognates of those will find their way onto the exams.
