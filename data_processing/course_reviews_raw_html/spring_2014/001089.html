This is the only course I've taken at Princeton that I would recommend, without hesitation, to any Princeton student. We all use language on a day-to-day basis, and hardly anybody ever thinks about what it is or how it got to be that way. This course covers both in a rigorous and eye-opening way while still managing to throw in oodles of fascinating trivia and general discussion. Students of humanities, social science, and engineering will all find much of interest here. That's not to say this is a perfect course: it isn't. Lectures (though spectacular in general) sometimes get sidetracked, precepts are less than they could be, and the problem sets and exams are occasionally frustrating (in addition to being moderately tough overall). Even so, it is very much worth your while to take this, no matter who you are at the university. Just make sure to sign up for it early-- Katz's courses fill up fast!
