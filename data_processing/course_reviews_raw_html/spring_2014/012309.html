One of the best courses I've taken at Princeton, better than COS424 for sure. Prof. Liu is really dedicated to making sure the students do well, and clearly has a good grasp on how to explain the material he is teaching. The lectures try to give you a sense of the fundamentals behind a number of statistical concepts and algorithms, and after finishing the class I really felt that I thoroughly understood things. The HW nicely complements this by looking at applications (e.g. spam filtering, image classification). You're given a piece of R-code to start with that essentially solves a simpler version of the HW problem, which you then have to 'tweak'. This way, you can really get in depth on the applications, and after this course I also felt confident I could deal with more complicated applications. Workload for HWs is very doable though, so don't worry about that. Beyond the homework, we only had a final, on which the open questions contained some curveballs, so beware. Finally, the TA's (especially Kevin Lin) are very helpful, respond quickly, and are clearly enthusiastic about the material. Overall, would very strongly recommend taking this class; you'll get a good understanding of some fundamental statistical concepts and algorithms, and will also work on some really cool applications.

Take it if you want to learn about statistics and machine learning.
This course will give a good overview of statistics and machine learning, with an emphasis on the frequentist approach. Take this if you are interested in how statistics and machine learning is used to approach big data sets in academia and industry. The course is structured into four parts: inferential analysis, predictive analysis, and exploratory analysis.
