At the beginning of the semester, I was really excited about this class -- I'd never taken psychology before, but it seemed pretty interesting (and relevant if you're WWS). Unfortunately, this class made us read a LOT of manipulated lab studies that bore no resemblance to the real world. Does playing Family Feud really tell us anything about how poor people make daily spending decisions? Almost certainly not. The grading is also pretty tough (read: arbitrary), and the exams are very difficult -- you have to know a lot about obscure studies we read and then don't discuss in lecture/precept. Lectures were extremely repetitive -- he reuses slides and reexplains concepts way too much. Also, this class takes a ton of jabs at traditional economics. Some of the criticisms are legitimate and interesting. I actually really enjoyed learning about prospect theory. Others are, as noted before, based on really silly methodology. But most frustrating of all was the amount of straw-manning that occurred -- a lot of attacks on "economics" turned out to be attacks on poorly understood representations of economics that few economists hold. That being said, Peter was a fantastic preceptor -- he's the reason I came out with some respect for psych, since he was willing to be critical of BS studies while pushing back on standard rational agent model criticisms.
