One concern I have with this class is that while this course is in theory like freshman writing sem in that it tries to get everyone on the same level for their JPs and senior thesis, there is a huge disparity between students' experiments in research. I don't think this is something that the professors can control, but outside of the typical lab course prereqs students needed to take to get here, if you have done research before and written up manuscripts on your results and such for an internship or whatever, you have a huge advantage over students who have not. That is not to say that you will not do well without experience, it's just that the overall methodology of doing a lit review for your intro, understanding how to present results and figures, and writing up a pertinent discussion, etc. are all things that are relatively novel (unfortunately due to the fact that our prereq lab courses have really inane and pointless lab write ups) for most students at this point. General advice for doing well in this class: 1. Go to every lecture. Take thorough notes. 2. Do lab write ups in your book before each session. The book is only 5% of your grade but honestly the benefits of staying on track with it are much greater and will help you on reports and such in the long run. Print out figures and put them in, too. 3.
