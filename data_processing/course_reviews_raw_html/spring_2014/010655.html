I'd recommend the class, but be prepared to be thrown off at first with the confusing notation and lectures.

The course is broken into two distinct halves. The first half is a normal lecture-weekly PSet format. The lectures are very very very very notation heavy (to the point that any non math/physics/engineer probably will never be able to reconcile with it) but the subject matter is pretty easy (again for a math-oriented person). Powell says in the first week that we should expect this to be about the difficulty and workload of ORF 245, which sounds about right. The content is interesting and somewhat applicable. The PSets are great - not time consuming and a nice change of pace after ORF 309, it is not Matlab heavy and you can figure out the programming as you go. Then, there is a midterm in week 7 that is hard to study for, since you are not really sure what you could be tested on. But you just look at the few example problems in the book and the test wasn't too hard (the cheat sheet was imperative for the first time in a class for me). The second half of the class is a whole different story. No psets, no final. Just lectures in the course that may or may not be helpful for the final project. Class attendence dropped and most of the lectures were not immediately understandable (and there were no psets to help us to). The project is very open ended without much direction - which is usually worse than it is good. Start simple and small with stuff that you learned. Only then do you bite off stuff from the second half lectures and even then keep your model simple. Also worth mentioning there were no precepts even though score made us sign up for one.
