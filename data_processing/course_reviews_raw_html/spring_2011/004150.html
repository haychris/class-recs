When you see that this course is titled "Linear Algebra" you might think that this course is easy. I certainly did. But don't be fooled by its deceivingly simple name. Linear Algebra is not about solving for an unknown variable in x 1 = 2. I mean, it's based on a similar concept, but it's different. Think about solving for an unknown vector instead. Not so bad? Well, now imagine that the vector in is one dimension, two dimension, three dimension, four dimension, five dimensions. Suddenly you can't visualize it any more. Then add the kernel, a complex contraption that requires you to find vectors perpendicular to other vectors, and everything becomes a jumbled mess. And that's only the tip of the iceberg. The exams ask you ridiculously hard true or false questions and ask you to do mini-proofs - justifications for all of your answers - and you have to provide very good explanations or you lose points. The midterms and finals are also ridiculously hard. They teach you a simple concept in class, they go two or three levels deeper on the exam and expect you to solve it. I guess the equivalent would be if somebody taught you the pythagorean theorem, but then on the test asked you to prove it. My advice is to go through ALL the midterm and final problems. The book is pretty useless as their examples are pretty elementary. But if you go thorugh all the problems you can get more practice for what the real tests are like.
