The exams aren't very hard, although there's a lot of memorization of dates of pieces and composers' births and deaths for no apparent reason. The best part is Simon Morrison's lectures, he makes everything very engaging and actually gives wonderfully interesting background stories about the composers and so forth. It's a bit disheartening to see people get up after the 50 minutes when he's in the middle of talking, but if you stick around you'll hear some neat information from a very much prominent musicologist. The lectures themselves are presented impeccably; he has a certain style and flair to his public speaking that most professors here lack completely. Overall, a very fun class if you put in a reasonable amount of work and go to the lectures and precepts.
