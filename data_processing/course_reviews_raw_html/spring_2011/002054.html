A very good course. If you are interested in COS and want more after 126 then definitely take it.
As far as COS classes go, this is pretty dull material. Well, maybe it isn't, but no effort is made to make it interesting. Professor Sedgewick is a nice guy and is very knowledgeable about the material. He organized the course incredibly well. However, his lectures are boring. He seems to have no regard for the students' level of engagement, and the relative dullness of the material. He spends an equal amount of time on each topic, which is frustrating since easy topics take just as long to get through as the hard ones, for which not enough time is devoted. He is pretty cocky about how much experience he has teaching the material, and about how well his book has sold. The textbook is very well-organized, but not written clearly. No one appreciated the fact that we were forced to go half the semester without a book, then buy it as soon as the new edition was released. The programming assignments were usually interesting and had a cool end-product. However, they were typically unrelated to what we were learning in lecture, other than a one-line call to an algorithm we might have learned. Be careful with the weekly exercises, as they end up making a huge impact on your grade. Definitely check them over for silly mistakes with other people beforehand.
Be prepared for a lot of competition from students and not a lot of help.
