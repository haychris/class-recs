It seems that a large part of what is required to do well in this class has nothing to do with understanding this class's material at all. Instead, due to the length of the exams in the amount of time allotted, computational speed in basic calculus plays a huge role in doing well. Additionally, there seems to be a mismatch between what is focused on in teaching and what is focused on in the exams, in that exams test corner cases or hinge on extremely specific concepts that were merely glazed over in both the lectures and the problem sets. Finally, the problem sets, despite their length, do not do a very good job of preparing you for the exam material. Professor Stone's gets high marks from many students, but personally, his style did not sink in for me. I found myself bogged down in specific steps on the blackboard and missing the idea of the overall approach. If you have to take this course, make sure you're computationally fast with your calculus -- not a big deal for most I'm sure, but it had been more than five years since I took calculus when I began the course.
