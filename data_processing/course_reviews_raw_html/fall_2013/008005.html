! I thought it was a good introduction to regression and time series. It's focused on the theory, though nothing too abstract, behind the generalized linear model, anova, some quantile and instrumental variable regression, logistic regression, time series (AR(p), MA(q), ARMA(p,q) models). Homework questions were usually "show that this is true" problems. Make sure your linear algebra is in good shape, specifically things like decompositions (eigen, spectral, whatever), positive definiteness, change of basis, idempotency. Also get used to differentiating and working with summations. The professor thought us stupid for our lack of linear algebra knowledge, and at other times said things like "this is so easy, even your little sister could do this." It was pretty funny actually, but in any case I don't think Professor Hallin will still be here next year.
