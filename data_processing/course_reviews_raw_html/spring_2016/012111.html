Courses like this that deal with highly theoretical yet also highly historically-contingent subjects run the strong risk of missing the forest for the trees or the trees for the forest. This course managed to miss both the forest and the trees, by going into no more depth and analysis of historical events in its kiddie pool-shallow lectures than a middle school history class, and by bringing up each reading at most once and then never discussing them again. It was immensely difficult to determine who "major theorists" were, given that we discussed every reading except Thucydides (seriously, not everything has to be compared to Ancient Greece, and if I have to hear or read one more bozo who insists that Greek civilization ended after the Peloponnesian War...historically illiterate statements like that were a frequent occurrence in lecture) exactly once. That lack of in-depth analysis makes the comical volume of reading assigned (fewer than 200 pages A MEETING was light, and for one meeting we were assigned an entire 500 page book and another 100 pages of supplemental readings) all the more unconscionable. In short do not take this class, especially if you are interested in strategy or history. The offensively shallow treatment of nearly every aspect of the subject and the mismatch between the depth and volume of the reading and the intellectual barrenness of anything in class, puts me in mind of a certain scene from from Billy Madison. A poor grade is a badge of honor.
