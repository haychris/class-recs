The secret to getting the most you can out of this course: You Should Do All the Readings. How is this to be done, you ask? Well, since the reading load is around 2-3 books per week due on Tuesday, Wednesday, Thursday, that last Thursday book can easily sneak up on you even if you have already read the Tuesday book over the weekend. The way I circumvented this problem was by tricking myself into thinking all the books were due Sunday night, so that I read them all during the prior week (which also allowed me to catch up/ postpone readings--yet still get them done by class time--if I had an exceptionally hard week). Not saying this is the best way, but in order to appreciate what this whole course is about/ not be bored in lecture and precept you should do the readings. Also, secret 1a is to always come to precept with a few points/ passages you want to discuss--not because that will impress your teachers, but because it will a) allow you to test out ideas for your essay and b) keep you engaged, interested and learning in precept.
