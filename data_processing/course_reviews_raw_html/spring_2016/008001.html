Of the required course for ORFE majors, ORF 335 is definitely the most interesting and applicable to some of the things that financial engineers will be doing in their careers. Professor Sircar teaches this course well. The course begins by learning how to price all kinds of options by coding in Matlab and learning the theory behind essential concepts like no-arbitrage and Black-Scholes. I found the first half of the semester to be much more concrete, more "real-world" than the latter half. The second half was much more theoretical and stressed proof-based math with some techniques similar to (although not quite as difficult as) ORF 309. I would definitely recommend the course to non-ORFE majors as well if you are looking to gain more experience with options and finance-related mathematical techniques. However, this course is very demanding. Take it if you are looking for an interesting challenge.
