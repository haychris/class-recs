TAKE. This course is genuinely fascinating, very light in workload, and generous in grading. That's the perfect combination for students wanting a fifth or sixth class. The problem sets take less than an hour each and there are only four of them. The median grade in the class is an A minus. Also, and most importantly, you learn a lot in this course and it's very cool stuff. I have friends who weren't interested in linguistics at all who took the course with me and now they're thinking they'll pursue the certificate. TIPS: If the course is full, email the professor and ask for a spot on the waitlist. The semester I took it they expanded the course and everyone who asked to be on the waitlist got in. Also make sure you spend a few hours studying for the midterm and learn IPA and your vowel chart well. It's the only assignment you'll have that isn't open book. When doing the fourth problem set make sure to put an asterisk before proto-words and proto-sounds.
