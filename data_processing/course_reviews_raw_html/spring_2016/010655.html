Although I was interested in the applications of operations research and machine learning, I found that the course taught concepts in an unusual way. You do learn about the process of information collection and decision-making, and the final project really allows you to actually come up with cool ideas. Also, the problem sets don't take up a lot of time at all.
Although Professor Powell claims that the course is somewhat similar (in terms of workload) to 245, this really reminded me more of 307 (in terms of the material, assignments. and project). Powell is a great presenter, though, and incorporates his own experience working on learning problems for various industries. Overall, this is a good course that teaches you how to think about decision making and problems, and while the final project is time consuming and can be extremely frustrating, it's good preparation for independent work/thesis. The programming component isn't too heavy (just consists mainly of modifying a few lines of code in MATLAB), and you don't need a programming background or be good at theory to do well in the course. The notation and the way information is presented in the book and in lecture can be off-putting at times, but just focus on understanding the underlying conceptual themes. Great team of preceptors too!
