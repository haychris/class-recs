ARC 577 / MOD 577   Graded A-F, P/D/F, Audit

Topics in Contemporary Architectural Theory - Building Postmodernism

Sylvia Lavin

Rather than emphasize postmodernity as a producer of theory or focus on a triangulation of its stylistic attributes, the seminar will use the building as a means to develop a new set of conjectures about what can be learned from the postmodern today. New approaches to construction, new design tools, forms of office organization and communications will be examined in the most exacting and prosaic detail.

Sample reading list:
Drolet, Michael, ed., The Postmodernism Reader: Foundational Texts
Foster, Hal, ed., The Anti-Aesthetic: Essays on Postmodern Culture
Wallis, Brian, ed., Art After Modernism: Rethinking Representation
Hays, K. Michael, ed., Architecture Theory Since 1968
See instructor for complete list

Reading/Writing assignments:
Assigned readings, completed before relevant class. Participation in assigned building presentations. Final paper/project.

Requirements/Grading:
Other (See Instructor) - 100%

Other Requirements:
Open to Graduate Students Only.

Other information:
Students will work in teams. Each team will present one case study per week. Every case study must include the following components: - A contextual history of the building - A formal analysis of the building - A detailed discussion of at least one primary object related to the building discussion of a text written within 10 years of the building?s construction date - A discussion of a text written after 2005
