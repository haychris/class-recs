* TWI 107   na, npdf

Intermediate Twi II

Hannah Essien

This course expands on the language skills acquired from TWI 105 and continues to focus on the communicative approach to studying the language. It includes specific socio-cultural settings and events. Speaking, reading, writing and listening continue to form an integral part of the course and students will build on their grammatical skills. By the end of the course, learners are expected to reach proficiency level ranging between Novice High and Intermediate Low.

Sample reading list:
Dolphyne, Florence Abena, A Comprehensive Course in Twi (Asante) for the Non-Twi Learn
Ofori, Seth, Ma Yenka Akan (Twi)
Gyamfua-Fofie, Akosua, Twi Akenkan Dwumadie
Kasahorow, Akan Learner's Dictionary: Akan-English, English-Akan (2012)

Reading/Writing assignments:
Weekly language lessons, including reading, audio practice, conversation, role-plays and written exercises. Quizzes and presentations.

Requirements/Grading:
Mid Term Exam - 20%
Final Exam - 20%
Quizzes - 20%
Other (See Instructor) - 40%

Prerequisites and Restrictions:
TWI 105.

Other information:
This is the fourth course in a four-term sequence. Other 40% = 10% attendance/class participation, 15% assignments and 15% group or individual projects.
