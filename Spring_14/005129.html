* PHY 104 (STL)   No Pass/D/Fail

General Physics II

Christopher G. Tully

This calculus-based course is primarily geared to students majoring in engineering and physics, but is also well suited to majors in other sciences. The goal of the course is to develop an understanding of the fundamental laws of physics, in particular, electricity and magnetism, with applications to electronics, optics, and new challenges in renewable energy sources.

Sample reading list:
Tipler and Mosca, Physics for Scientists and Engineers 6th edition Volume 2

Reading/Writing assignments:
Weekly 1 hour lecture participation. Weekly reading of 1-2 chapters. Weekly problem sets (2-3 hours). Weekly 3 hour lab. Three precept sessions per week. Tri-weekly in-class 1 hour exams.

Requirements/Grading:
Final Exam - 30%
Quizzes - 20%
Lab Reports - 15%
Class/Precept Participation - 3%
Problem set(s) - 32%

Prerequisites and Restrictions:
PHY 103 or PHY 105 or permission of instructor..

Equivalent Courses:
PHY 104, PHY 102, PHY 106 are considered equivalent courses and cannot be taken multiple times for credit.

Other information:
Community Based Learning Initiative (CBLI) option is offered. WebAssign will NOT be used for this course.
